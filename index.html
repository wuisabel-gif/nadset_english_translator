About the machine

Nadsat isn’t a full language — it’s English with roughly 250 slang words mixed in, most adapted from Russian, plus a few from German, French, Malay and Romani, some Cockney rhyming slang, and childish coinages. So this translator works by substitution: multi-word terms first, then each word looked up with its plural and verb endings carried across; anything unknown passes through untouched.

Honest limits: output is lower-cased where the engine has no better guess, verb endings follow simple rules (so a few forms differ from Burgess’s), and where one English word has several Nadsat synonyms the machine picks one. Hover any orange word to see its root.
